A client has just signed with your firm. They're excited, reassured, ready to hand you their books.

Then they get your first onboarding email:

"Hello, please send us your registration certificate, your up-to-date articles of association, bank details, the partners' ID documents, last year's accounts… You can reply to this email with everything."

And just like that, their great first impression cracks. You've turned a moment of trust into administrative drudgery.

A firm's first impression isn't made at the signing meeting

It's made at this exact moment: the first time the client has to do something for you.

If they have to dig through their files, scan in a rush, guess which format you want, and pile ten attachments into an email, you're sending an involuntary signal: "working with us is going to be paperwork."

A modern firm can no longer afford that. The market is tight, clients compare, and the onboarding experience is one of the rare places where you can stand out before you've produced a single set of accounts.

A branded portal, opened in one click

Picture the other version.

The client receives a single link. They open it — on their phone, on the train, with nothing to install and no account to create. They land on a clean portal, branded as your firm (your logo, your colors, your welcome message).

The portal tells them, step by step, exactly which documents to upload:

  • Registration certificate
  • Articles of association
  • Bank details
  • Partners' ID documents

On each upload, the system quietly checks that the format is right and that the expected document is actually there. The client has no decision to make, no question to ask. They upload, they confirm, they're done.

On your side, the file arrives complete and structured in your operator inbox, ready to be assigned to the lead team member and exported to your production software.

That's the first impression you want to leave: "with this firm, even the paperwork is simple."

This is exactly what a tool like IntakeFlow enables: a private link with no login, a guided portal in your brand, and an inbox to process it all.

And the portal keeps you "GDPR-clean"

This isn't just a branding concern. It's a compliance one too — a sensitive point for a partner accountable for the file.

Collecting ID documents and financial data by email means:

  • personal data scattered across individual mailboxes,
  • no traceability of who accessed what,
  • no clean way to purge the documents once the file is closed.

A structured collection portal changes the game. Documents are centralized instead of scattered, downloadable and purgeable from the console (GDPR purge of attachments), and the processing history is tracked.
